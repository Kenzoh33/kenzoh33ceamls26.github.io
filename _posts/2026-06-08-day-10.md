  Today was full of research. Our session started with a discussion of where we left off on Friday, and then we began our individual tasks. I completed my assignment on unimodal models. While researching the content, I got to learn about different ways in which Unimodal is implemented. After that, we had to complete our 15 domain predictions for the multimodal assignment, where I learned to predict the outcome of two domains based on their properties and the information it carries. I also got to learn about different features that make two domains complementary and redundant.

  After that, we had an individual project to build our own final fusion model to use complementary domains from unimodal experimentation and compare the two results. Building this final fusion model for complementary domains taught us a lot about AUC, F-1 Score, Confusion matrix, and precision. These features determine how a model is performing, and knowing them exactly helps a lot while building a model. As we were waiting for confirmation of our dataset, we practiced further about preprocessing datasets to remove noise and filter them through a demo dataset. I learned a lot about how datasets are pre-processed, without actually doing any calculations on them, through this.
