what_i_learned: |
  We had our literature review workshop session with Dr. Anita Pandey to begin our day. In this session, I learned about different ways to make research papers stand out and be more appealing. I learned how picking a captivating title plays a vital role in attracting an audience towards the research. We also learned about the formulated ways of research paper writing: Abstract, Introduction, Relevant Work, Methodology, Discussion, and Conclusion. We also got insight about Rayyan, which is a web app designed for systematic research reviews. Knowing about the tools used by professionals in their field is always useful and helpful.

  After that, we started our research labs, which began by exploring the ECG datasets as usual. I ran my code again to generate a report indifferent domains of the ECG signal to find which one is the most complementary with the highest fusion gain. My model found out that the Time and Frequency domain as a pair is the most compatible domain with the highest fusion gain and uniqueness to the information. We were also assigned to complete a student report based on this exploration, where we had to explain what were the results of different domains measured together. Overall, we came to the conclusion that the time and Frequency domains are always the most compatible ones, and where our focus should be moving ahead.
